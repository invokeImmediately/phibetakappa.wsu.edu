Visiting Scholar Program

The Gamma Chapter of Phi Beta Kappa brings to the Pullman campus exceptional guest speakers thanks to the Visiting Scholar Program in the national organization.

Jeremy A. Sabloff (2016)

Phi Beta Kappa Visiting Scholar Jeremy A. Sabloff, an internationally renowned anthropologist, will visit the Pullman campus for a guest lecture and visits with students on Oct. 6-7.

He is the external professor and past president of the Santa Fe Institute (2009-2015), and Christopher H. Browne Distinguished Professor of Anthropology Emeritus at the University of Pennsylvania. He is an expert on ancient Maya civilization and pre-industrial urbanism.

He earned his bachelor's degree at the University of Pennsylvania, and his Ph.D. from Harvard University. He is a member of the National Academy of Sciences and is a fellow in the American Association for the Advancement of Science.

William A. Wulf (2009)

During an April 16-17 visit, computer scientist and entrepreneur William A. Wulf delivered a lecture and visited with students.

Co-sponsors of the visit werethe School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Visiting Lecture Series and the Office of Undergraduate Education.

Wulf is the former president of the National Academy of Engineering (NAE) and an assistant director of the National Science Foundation (NSF), and is the AT&T Professor of Engineering and Applied Science at the University of Virginia, Charlottesville.
